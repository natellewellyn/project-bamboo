Language Domain Analysis

Average 2025-26 ACCESS scores by skill domain · cohort-wide

Writing is the weakest domain at an average of 2.98, compared to the strongest domain (Speaking: 3.69). The Listening–Writing gap of 0.50 points signals students understand English better than they can produce written text — a common pattern suggesting the program should prioritize structured writing practice across all grade levels.
Action: Target explicit writing instruction — sentence frames, paragraph organization, and academic vocabulary — especially for LTEL and mid-level students (composite 2.5–3.5).

LTEL vs Active EL Performance

Long-term EL students (5+ years) compared to general cohort

Avg ACCESS Composite · Avg GPA · ELA % Met Standard

LTEL students are significantly underperforming across all metrics. Their avg composite is 3.38 vs 3.32 for the broader cohort, and their avg GPA is 1.58 vs 2.18. Not one LTEL student met the ELA standard on RISE.
Recommendations for LTEL students:
• Develop individualized LTEL redesignation plans with annual benchmarks
• Shift instruction toward content-area academic language, not basic conversational EL support
• Prioritize oral reading fluency and academic writing — the domains where LTEL students most lag
• Involve families in goal-setting meetings; chronic absence (common in this group) is a compounding factor
